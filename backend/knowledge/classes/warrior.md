# Warrior

## Overview
The Warrior is PoE2's pure Strength-based melee class. Built around massive physical hits, active blocking, stun, armour break, warcries, and deliberate weighty combat. The Warrior rewards patience and positioning — every attack is a commitment, every hit is impactful. This is not a fast class, and that is intentional. The Warrior class will now be your go to if you plan on playing Mace builds.

Best suited to players who enjoy frontline combat, tanking through defences, and delivering devastating single hits rather than rapid attack chains.

---

## Playstyle
Warrior gameplay is methodical and deliberate. You position, set up a combo — often a warcry into an armour break into a slam — then deliver a devastating hit followed by chained aftershocks or explosions. Movement and timing matter more than raw spam. Boss fights require reading telegraphs, blocking dangerous hits with your shield, then punishing openings with heavy slams. Clear speed is below average compared to Ranger or Sorceress, but single-target damage and survivability are among the highest of any class.

## Usual Attributes
- **Primary:** Strength
- **Secondary:** Dexterity (minor), Intelligence (minor)

---

## Key Mechanics
- **Stun** — Large hits build stun buildup on enemies. Fully stunned enemies are briefly interrupted, creating openings for follow-up slams. See: `mechanics/combat/stun.md`
- **Armour Break** — Reduces an enemy's armour, amplifying all physical damage taken. Core to advanced Warrior combo rotations. See: `mechanics/combat/armour_break.md`
- **Warcries** — Active skills that buff the Warrior, debuff enemies, taunt, or detonate nearby corpses. Seismic Cry and Infernal Cry are the primary warcries. Warcries are central to the Warbringer ascendancy and important utility for all Warriors.
- **Active Blocking** — Shields block attacks when the player actively guards. Warrior benefits enormously from this — correct block timing can negate devastating boss hits entirely.

---

## What This Class Is Not
- **Not a fast mapper.** Do not pick Warrior expecting Ranger-speed clears. The class is strong but deliberate.
- **Not a glass cannon.** Warrior is designed around layered defences — armour, block, life, stun. Building purely for damage and ignoring survivability will feel terrible.
- **Not PoE1 Marauder.** The Warrior has been redesigned. Mechanics like warcries, armour break, and active blocking are distinct from their PoE1 equivalents. Do not assume PoE1 knowledge transfers directly.

## Ascendancies

### Titan
The slam specialist. Titan amplifies the Warrior's core identity — bigger hits, more stun, larger AoE, and enhanced survivability. The most straightforward Warrior ascendancy and the recommended choice for beginners. Excellent for bosses. Currently considered one of the strongest melee setups in the Early Access meta.
**Difficulty:** Beginner-friendly
**Rewards:** Players who want maximum impact from every slam, strong boss presence, and clear passive scaling.

### Warbringer
The tactical warcry fighter. Warbringer builds around warcries as a primary damage and utility source — detonating corpses, chaining explosions, dropping totems, and controlling the battlefield. More mechanically complex than Titan, rewarding active play and combo execution.
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Rewards:** Players who enjoy layered gameplay, buffs, debuffs, and battlefield control over pure slam damage.

### Smith of Kitava
The defensive specialist. Smith of Kitava trades offensive ascendancy nodes for greatly enhanced survivability — increased maximum resistances, life scaling, and strength bonuses. The key mechanic (Smith's Masterwork) forces a Normal rarity body armour but lets you engrave powerful mods directly onto it. Gearing is easier due to resistance nodes, making this excellent for Hardcore and Solo Self-Found.
**Difficulty:** Beginner-friendly
**Rewards:** Players who prioritise survival, Hardcore players, SSF players, and anyone frustrated by dying.
**Note:** Community opinion of the Warrior improved significantly after Smith of Kitava was introduced.

## Starting Weapon
**Two-Handed Mace** (default starting weapon)
The Warrior starts with a two-handed mace. This is the most natural weapon for Warrior gameplay — high physical damage, strong stun scaling, and access to the full slam skill set. Players can transition to a one-handed mace and shield for more defence, or experiment with other weapon types, but two-handed mace is the most supported and recommended path through the campaign.

## Passive Tree Start
The Warrior starts in the **bottom-left** of the passive tree, in the Strength cluster. The starting area contains:
- Increased physical damage with maces and two-handed weapons
- Life and armour nodes
- Stun threshold and stun buildup nodes
- Strength attribute nodes (which further increase life and physical damage)

The Warrior's starting position is well-connected to:
- The central life cluster (moving right toward the centre of the tree)
- The armour and block cluster (moving upward toward the Marauder/Duelist border)
- Keystone nodes: **Giant's Blood** (allows using a two-handed weapon stat in your off-hand), **Resolute Technique** (never miss, cannot crit), **Avatar of Fire** (converts physical to fire)

## Gear Priorities

The following affix priorities apply to all Warrior builds. For crafting advice, prioritise **prefix slots** for life and physical damage, **suffix slots** for resistances and attributes.

### Weapon (Two-Handed Mace)
**Critical affixes:**
- `X to Y Physical Damage` (prefix) — the single most important stat. Upgrade your weapon every few levels.
- `% Increased Physical Damage` (prefix) — multiplies base damage, very strong.
- `Increased Attack Speed` (suffix) — dramatically improves feel and DPS.

**Secondary affixes:**
- `Accuracy Rating` — Warriors miss more than expected on slow attacks. Accuracy matters.
- `Adds X to Y Physical Damage` — flat damage added to attacks.

### Helmet
**Critical affixes:**
- `Maximum Life` (prefix)
- `Fire/Cold/Lightning Resistance` (suffix) — prioritise whichever resistance is lowest.

**Secondary affixes:**
- `Strength` — attributes double-dip into life and damage.
- `Armour` (prefix)

### Chest
**Critical affixes:**
- `Maximum Life` (prefix) — largest life pool item slot.
- `Resistances` (suffix) — chest can carry two resistance suffixes efficiently.

**Secondary affixes:**
- `Armour` (prefix)
- `Strength`

**Note for Smith of Kitava:** Must use a Normal (white) rarity body armour. The ascendancy engraves mods onto it directly — do not upgrade the rarity of your chest armour.

### Boots
**Critical affixes:**
- `Maximum Life` (prefix)
- `Movement Speed` (suffix) — Warrior is slow. Movement speed is more valuable than on faster classes.
- `Resistances` (suffix)

### Rings / Amulet / Belt
**Critical affixes:**
- Resistances to reach the 75% cap on Fire, Cold, and Lightning
- `Maximum Life`

**Secondary affixes:**
- `Strength`
- `Accuracy Rating` (ring or amulet)
- `Physical Damage` (amulet prefix)

### Flask Priority
Warrior uses one health flask and one mana flask.
- **Health flask:** Largest base life recovery available. `Surgeon's` prefix (recharges on crit) or `Seething` (instant recovery) for emergency healing.
- **Mana flask:** Small recovery is enough — Warriors use mana for warcries and some slams. `Chemist's` prefix (reduced charges used) extends duration.
- **Charms (up to 3):** Prioritise freeze immunity, bleed immunity, and a utility charm of choice.

## Common Beginner Mistakes
- **Neglecting weapon upgrades.** A Warrior with an outdated weapon feels terrible. Upgrade every 4–5 levels minimum.
- **Building only for damage.** Without life, armour, and resistances, even strong Warriors die quickly in Act 2+.
- **Expecting fast clear speed.** Warrior is methodical. Frustration from comparing clear speed to Ranger or Sorceress is very common.
- **Ignoring resistances.** Resistances drop below 75% cap at Act 2 and Act 3 difficulty transitions. Players often die suddenly and don't know why — it is almost always resistances.
- **Not blocking.** Active blocking is a major Warrior tool. Players who forget to block waste a significant portion of the class's defensive kit.
- **Ignoring accuracy.** Slow attacks that miss are devastating. Check accuracy rating in the character sheet — aim to stay above 85%.
- **Skipping movement skills.** Warrior without movement tools feels sluggish and gets surrounded. Rolling Slam or dodge roll usage is essential.
- **Waiting too long to use warcries.** Warcries have cooldowns but are powerful. New players often forget to use them at all.
