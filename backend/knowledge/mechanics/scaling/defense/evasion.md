# Evasion

## Overview
Evasion gives you a chance to completely avoid damage from enemy Strikes and Projectiles. When you Evade, you take zero damage from that hit. Evasion applies to both Attacks and Spells that are delivered via Strike or Projectile.

---

## How Evasion Works
- Provides a percentage chance to completely negate a hit
- Applies to Strikes (melee) and Projectiles
- Does NOT apply to Area of Effect damage by default
- Each Evasion check is independent — you can Evade multiple hits in a row, or fail to Evade multiple in a row (streaky by nature)

---

## Scaling Evasion

**Passive Tree**
- Evasion nodes near Dexterity starting areas (Ranger, Shadow positions)
- "% Increased Evasion Rating" nodes and notables

**Gear**
- Base Evasion on Dexterity-based gear
- "% Increased Evasion Rating" prefix on gear

**Skills**
- **Wind Dancer** — increases your Evasion rating

---

## Keystones

### Acrobatics
- Allows you to Evade Area of Effect damage — normally AoE bypasses Evasion entirely
- **Trade-off:** Severely reduces your chance to Evade other hits
- Only worthwhile when heavily invested into Evasion and specifically building around AoE avoidance

### Iron Reflexes
- Converts ALL Evasion into additional Armour
- Removes Evasion (you can no longer Evade) but greatly increases Armour
- Good for Strength/Dexterity hybrid builds that want a large Armour pool without pure Strength gear

---

## Evasion vs Armour
- Evasion is binary — you either avoid the hit entirely or take full damage
- Armour is consistent — always reduces physical damage from every hit
- Evasion is stronger against frequent small hits (more chances to Evade)
- Armour is more reliable against rare large hits
