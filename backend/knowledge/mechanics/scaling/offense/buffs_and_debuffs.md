# Buffs and Debuffs

## Persistent Buffs (Spirit Cost)
Reserve Spirit to maintain constant damage buffs that are always active while Spirit is available.

- Examples: Attrition, Archmage, Berserk
- Any Curse can be socketed into Blasphemy to convert it into a Persistent Buff at Spirit cost — applies the curse aura to all nearby enemies automatically
- Spirit is a limited resource — balance persistent buffs against other Spirit costs

---

## Curses
Curses apply debuffs to all enemies within radius after a short delay.

**Key rules:**
- Only **one Curse** can be active on an enemy by default
- This limit can be raised through specific gear affixes and passive tree nodes
- Curses reduce enemy resistances or cause enemies to take increased damage

**Examples:**
- Flammability — reduces Fire Resistance
- Vulnerability — enemies take increased Physical damage
- Conductivity — reduces Lightning Resistance
- Frostbite — reduces Cold Resistance

---

## Marks
Marks target a single enemy and grant miscellaneous effects.

**Key rules:**
- Marks can only be active against **one enemy at a time**
- Marks are **separate from the Curse limit** — you can always apply one Mark regardless of how many Curses are active
- Only one Mark can be active at a time

**Examples:**
- Sniper's Mark — projectile effects on marked target
- Voltaic Mark — lightning-related effects on marked target

---

## Temporary Buffs
Active for a fixed duration or number of skill uses. More powerful than persistent buffs but require active management.

- **Mana Tempest** — Grants massive Gained Damage while standing inside it, but rapidly drains mana. Positioning-dependent.
- **Barrage** — Empowers the next Bow Attack to repeat multiple times. Incurs a large cooldown after use.
- **Pain Offering** — Grants a large DPS bonus to minions for a duration.
- **Seismic Cry** — Warcry that causes the next attack to create a guaranteed Shockwave, provided the Warcry hit a heavy stunned enemy.

## Debuffs (Enemy Takes More Damage)

**Critical rule:** Sources of "increased damage taken" are **additive with each other**. Stacking too many of the same debuff type results in diminishing returns — the same rule as stacking "increased" damage modifiers.

### Exposure
- Reduces enemy elemental resistances by 20% (default)
- Sources: Frost Bomb, Lightning Exposure, and various skills/items
- Cannot stack — only one active per element

### Wither
- Stacks up to 10 times, each stack = +5% Chaos Damage taken
- Maximum: 50% increased Chaos Damage taken at 10 stacks
- Particularly valuable for Chaos damage builds

### Shock
- Default effect: enemy takes **20% increased damage** from all sources
- Chance to Shock scales with the size of the hit relative to enemy life
- Shock magnitude can be increased with "Increased Magnitude of Shock you inflict"
- Because Shock increases ALL damage taken (not just Lightning), it is valuable for any damage type

### Intimidate
- Causes affected enemy to take **10% increased damage**
- Example source: Blackheart (unique ring)
- Additive with other increased damage taken debuffs
